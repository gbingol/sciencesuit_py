Food:aw

Calculates the water activity of the food material.

Syntax:

aw(arg) → number

  1. If arg is not provided, then predictive equations will be used.
  2. If arg is a number (0,1], then aw=num regardless of composition.
  3. If arg is a function, then aw=func(food)

The following assumptions were made:
1) %Water < 0.00001 → aw=0.01
2) %Solute <1 → aw=0.99


For non-electrolyte solutions (% salt < 0.00001):
1) %water > 99.0 → aw=0.98
2) %CHO>98 and all others <1% → aw=0.70
3) %water (1,5) and %CHO > 5 → aw=MoneyBorn equation
4) %water >5 and %CHO>5 → aw=Norrish equation

  1. MoneyBorn and Norrish equation: Fructoseamount = CHOamount.
    2. >
  2. Norrish equation: Glycerolamount=lipidamount and alanineamount=proteinamount

Example:

>>milk=Food.new{water=88.13, protein=3.15, CHO=4.80, lipid=3.25, ash=0.67}

>>milk:aw()
0.92
